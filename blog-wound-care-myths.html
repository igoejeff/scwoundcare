5 Common Myths About Chronic Wound Care (Debunked)

March 2026 6 min read Carolina Wound Care Team Aiken & Charleston, SC

If you've been living with a wound that won't heal, you've probably heard all kinds of advice from well-meaning friends and family — or even from the internet. "Just let it air out." "It'll heal on its own." "Try this cream I saw online."

The problem? A lot of that advice is actually a myth. These misconceptions can delay real healing and leave you feeling more frustrated. At Carolina Wound Care, we hear these myths every week from patients in Aiken, Charleston, and across South Carolina. The good news? Once we clear them up, people feel hopeful and ready to move forward.

Let's debunk the top 5 together.

MYTH 1

"It will heal on its own if I just wait longer."

The Truth

Chronic wounds that haven't improved in 4–6 weeks usually need specialized help. Waiting longer often lets the wound get "stuck" and raises the risk of infection or serious complications. Early expert care almost always leads to faster, safer healing — and prevents problems that become much harder to treat down the line.

MYTH 2

"Keep the wound dry and open to the air."

The Truth

Modern wound care science is clear: a moist wound environment actually helps new skin grow faster and reduces scarring. Letting a wound dry out forms a hard scab that slows cell migration across the surface — and can cause more pain. The right professional dressings create the perfect healing conditions your wound needs to progress.

MYTH 3

"Over-the-counter creams or home remedies are enough."

The Truth

Drugstore products and home remedies might help minor scrapes heal, but they don't address the underlying reasons a chronic wound won't heal — poor circulation, diabetes, pressure, infection, or nutritional gaps. Specialized wound care goes far beyond creams. It treats the whole person, identifies every contributing factor, and applies targeted treatments that actually make a difference.

MYTH 4

"If it doesn't hurt, it's not serious."

The Truth

Many chronic wounds — especially diabetic foot ulcers — involve nerve damage, so they can be very serious without causing significant pain. A painless wound is not a safe wound. Lack of pain doesn't mean lack of problems — it may actually mean the nerve damage is more severe. Regular professional checks catch issues early, before they become painful, dangerous, or lead to hospitalization.

MYTH 5

"Specialized wound care is only for severe cases or after everything else fails."

The Truth

The earlier you see a wound specialist, the better the outcome. You don't have to "try everything else" first or wait until things get severe. Many patients tell us they wish they had called sooner because expert care prevented complications and dramatically sped up healing. Early specialist involvement is not a last resort — it's the smartest first step.

The Bottom Line

Myths about wound care are common — and completely understandable. Most people are just trying to help. But when it comes to a wound that isn't healing, the right information can make the difference between weeks and months of recovery. When in doubt, call a specialist. That's exactly what we're here for.
