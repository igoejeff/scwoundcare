What We Offer

Comprehensive Wound Care Under One Roof — Or Your Roof

At Carolina Wound Care, every treatment is delivered by a board-certified wound specialist and backed by clinical evidence. We don't take a one-size-fits-all approach — we create personalized care plans for every patient.

Treatment 1

Advanced Debridement

Debridement is the removal of necrotic (dead), infected, or damaged tissue from a wound to allow healthy tissue to grow. It is one of the foundational steps in treating any chronic or non-healing wound.

Our specialists select the optimal debridement method based on your wound type, including sharp/surgical, enzymatic, autolytic, and mechanical techniques.

Clinical Evidence: According to the Wound Healing Society, regular debridement significantly accelerates healing in chronic wounds by removing biofilm and stimulating the wound bed's healing response.
Schedule Debridement Consultation

Debridement Methods We Use

  • Sharp/Surgical Debridement — Precise removal of necrotic tissue using sterile instruments
  • Enzymatic Debridement — Topical agents that digest necrotic tissue selectively
  • Autolytic Debridement — Using the body's own enzymes and moisture balance
  • Mechanical Debridement — Irrigation and wet-to-dry techniques when appropriate

Who Benefits From Grafts & Biologics?

  • Wounds that haven't responded to standard dressings after 4+ weeks
  • Large or deep diabetic foot ulcers
  • Venous leg ulcers with slow healing trajectory
  • Post-surgical wounds at risk of chronic non-healing
Treatment 2

Specialized Grafts & Biologics

When standard wound care isn't producing results, biological skin substitutes and grafts can provide the biological scaffolding needed to jump-start healing. These advanced treatments use growth factors and extracellular matrix components that the body needs to regenerate tissue.

Carolina Wound Care has deep expertise in applying and managing biologic wound care products — and we can do so at your home or facility.

Ask About Biologic Options
Treatment 3

Specialized Wound Dressings

Not all dressings are equal. The right dressing choice is critical — the wrong one can slow healing, increase infection risk, or cause additional tissue damage. Our specialists select from a comprehensive range of advanced dressings including antimicrobial, bioactive, moisture-balancing, and offloading options.

We reassess the dressing regimen at every visit and adjust as your wound progresses through the healing stages.

Get a Wound Assessment

Dressing Categories We Use

  • Antimicrobial dressings — Silver, iodine, PHMB to control bioburden
  • Moisture-balancing dressings — Hydrogels, foams, alginates for wound environment optimization
  • Collagen dressings — Support new tissue formation in chronic wounds
  • Negative pressure therapy (VAC) — For select wound types requiring enhanced drainage
According to the American College of Phlebology: Compression therapy is the gold standard for venous leg ulcer treatment. Combined with wound care, it reduces healing time by 50% or more compared to wound care alone.
Treatment 4

Compression Therapy

For venous leg ulcers and lymphedema-related wounds, compression therapy is the cornerstone of effective treatment. By improving circulation and reducing edema, compression creates the conditions that allow venous wounds to finally heal.

Our specialists apply and educate patients on multi-layer compression bandaging, compression stockings, and wrapping systems — all at your home or our clinic.

Learn About Venous Ulcers
Treatment 5

Personalized Care Plans

No two wounds are alike. Every patient at Carolina Wound Care receives a fully individualized, documented wound care plan that takes into account their wound type, stage, health history, medications, nutrition, and goals.

Your care plan evolves with your healing. We reassess at every visit and share updates with your primary care provider, specialist, or facility nursing staff through our InteliWound documentation system.

Request a Care Plan Consultation

What's Included in Your Care Plan

  • Comprehensive wound assessment with measurements and photos
  • Wound classification and healing stage documentation
  • Treatment protocol selection with rationale
  • Nutrition and offloading recommendations
  • Progress tracking and plan updates at every visit
  • Communication with your primary care team and family
