Credentials & Certifications

What Board-Certification in Wound Care Actually Means

Not every nurse or clinician who treats wounds holds a dedicated wound care certification. Here's why it matters for your healing.

The Wound Care Certified (WCC) and Wound Care Specialist (WCS) credentials are granted by the National Alliance of Wound Care and Ostomy (NAWCO) and the American Board of Wound Management (ABWM) respectively. These certifications require specialized education, clinical hours, and rigorous examination in wound management — far beyond a general nursing license.
The CWCN credential is issued by the Wound, Ostomy and Continence Nursing Certification Board (WOCNCB). It requires a registered nursing license, specialized wound care education, clinical experience, and a certification exam. Certified Wound Care Nurses are expert practitioners in the assessment, treatment, and prevention of chronic wounds.
Board-certified wound care specialists have demonstrated advanced knowledge of wound pathophysiology, evidence-based treatment protocols, and complex wound management — knowledge that general practitioners and home health aides typically do not have. Studies consistently show that patients cared for by certified wound specialists have better healing outcomes and lower complication rates.
InteliWound is an advanced electronic wound documentation and tracking system used by specialized wound care practices. Certification in InteliWound means our clinicians are trained in evidence-based documentation, wound measurement, and outcome tracking — ensuring precise, consistent care across every visit.
