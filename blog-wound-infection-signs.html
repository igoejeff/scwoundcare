Signs of Wound Infection: When to Call for Help Right Away

March 2026 8 min read Carolina Wound Care Team Aiken & Charleston, SC

If your wound suddenly feels hotter, looks redder, or starts draining more than usual, it's natural to feel concerned. You might wonder: "Is this normal, or is something wrong?"

The truth is that catching a wound infection early makes all the difference — and most infections are easily managed when you know exactly what to watch for. At Carolina Wound Care, we help patients across South Carolina feel confident and safe by teaching clear warning signs and providing fast, caring support the moment you need it.

Why Wound Infections Happen

Any open wound can become infected if bacteria enter the area. Chronic wounds — those that haven't healed in weeks — are especially vulnerable because they stay open longer. Factors like diabetes, poor circulation, or a weakened immune system can make infection more likely.

The good news? You and your care team can spot problems quickly and stop them from getting worse. Knowledge is protection.

Who Is Most at Risk?

Patients with diabetes, venous insufficiency, or compromised immune systems are at higher risk for wound infections. If you fall into any of these groups, daily wound checks are especially important — and our team is just one phone call away.

Common Signs of Wound Infection

Trust your instincts and check your wound daily. Contact us right away if you notice any of these changes:

1. Increased Redness or Streaking

Redness that spreads beyond the wound edges, or red lines extending toward the heart (streaking) — this is a sign bacteria may be spreading and needs immediate attention.

2. Swelling or Warmth

The area around the wound feels puffy, tight, or noticeably warmer than the surrounding skin — beyond normal healing inflammation.

3. More Pain or Tenderness

Pain that suddenly gets worse, throbs, or is present even when the wound is resting and not being touched. Healing wounds should gradually hurt less — not more.

4. Change in Drainage

More fluid than usual, thicker pus (yellow, green, or gray coloring), or a strong, foul odor coming from the wound. Clear or light pink drainage during normal healing is expected — this is different.

5. Fever or Chills

A temperature over 100.4°F (38°C), feeling hot and cold, or having general body aches — these can all signal your body is fighting a spreading infection.

6. New or Worsening Fatigue

Feeling unusually tired or unwell even if the wound looks the same on the surface — systemic fatigue can be an early sign that infection is affecting the whole body.

Even One Sign Is Enough to Call

You don't need to see all six of these warning signs to reach out. Even one of them is worth a call. It's always better to check early than to wait and let things progress. Call us at 866-540-8090 — we're here for you.

Why Acting Quickly Matters

A small infection can spread fast — delaying healing, causing more pain, or leading to bigger problems. The earlier we step in, the simpler and faster the solution usually is. Simple Steps You Can Take Between Visits

These easy habits protect your wound and help you spot problems quickly:

  • Wash your hands before and after touching the wound
  • Keep the area clean and dry — follow the instructions we give you exactly
  • Change dressings exactly as taught, on schedule
  • Protect the wound from bumps, pressure, or friction
  • Check the wound daily in good lighting — look for any of the 6 warning signs above

Write It Down

Keep a simple daily log of how your wound looks and feels — even just a few words or a photo on your phone. This makes it easy to spot changes early and gives our team valuable information at each visit.
